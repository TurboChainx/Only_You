Privacy Policy — Only You

Last updated: March 31, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how the Only You mobile application (the “App,” package name com.onlyyouchat.app) collects, uses, and shares information when you use the App.

The App is operated by the developer identified on the Google Play store listing for Only You (“we,” “us,” or “our”). By using the App, you agree to this policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

1. Information we collect

1.1 Account and profile information

When you register or use the App, we collect:

  • Email address
  • Phone number
  • Name or display name
  • Password (stored using secure hashing; we do not store plain-text passwords)
  • Profile details you choose to provide, including photos if you use camera or gallery features

1.2 Chat and AI interactions

To provide AI-powered conversations, we process:

  • Messages you send in chat sessions
  • Session and conversation data needed to show chat history and operate the service

Your messages are sent to our backend servers and may be transmitted to OpenAI (or another AI provider we configure) to generate responses. OpenAI’s handling of data is described in their privacy policy: https://openai.com/policies/privacy-policy.

1.3 Device and technical data

We may collect limited technical information such as device type, operating system version, app version, and diagnostic or error data needed to fix bugs and protect the service.

1.4 Permissions

The App may request Android permissions including camera, photos, microphone, SMS, phone, and contacts—only where used for the features described in the App. You can revoke permissions in system settings.

2. How we use information

We use the information to operate accounts, provide AI chat, store chat history as implemented, secure and improve the App, and comply with law. We do not sell your personal information.

3. Where data is stored and transferred

Account, chat, and app data are stored on servers running our backend API and MongoDB database. Data may be processed where OpenAI and our hosting providers operate, including the United States.
